# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### ✅ 5 Do's for Responsible Use
1. **Do adjust your privacy settings:** Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information.
2. **Do think before you post:** Remember that the internet is forever; employers and academic institutions frequently search online footprints.
3. **Do showcase your professional interests:** Use platforms to highlight academic achievements, coding projects, or tech interests.
4. **Do untag yourself from unprofessional content:** Protect your image by removing your name from inappropriate photos or threads.
5. **Do verify before you share:** Cross-check facts using reliable sources to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.

### ❌ 5 Don'ts to Avoid
1. **Don't post complaints about professors or peers:** Venting about university staff or classmates online can lead to disciplinary action or damaged reputations.
2. **Don't overshare sensitive data:** Never publicly post your exact location, class schedule, phone number, or address.
3. **Don't engage in toxic arguments:** Avoid getting dragged into heated, emotional debates in public comment sections.
4. **Don't use unprofessional language:** Offensive humor or inappropriate language can easily be screenshotted and shared out of context.
5. **Don't ignore platform security:** Don't reuse passwords across different social media accounts, and never leave accounts logged in on public campus computers.
