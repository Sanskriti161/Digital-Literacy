\# Case Study: UPI "Receive Money" Scam

\*\*What the crime is:\*\* UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a prevalent financial cybercrime in India where scammers manipulate victims into authorizing unintended money transfers using social engineering.

\*\*How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):\*\* 1. A scammer targets a victim—often on an online marketplace like OLX where a student is selling old textbooks or a laptop.

2\. The scammer quickly agrees to the asking price to build trust.

3\. The scammer sends a QR code or a "collect request" link to the student's UPI app, falsely claiming that scanning the code or entering their UPI PIN is required to \*receive\* the payment.

4\. To prevent the student from thinking critically, the scammer creates a false sense of urgency, insisting the transaction must be approved immediately.

5\. The student, rushing and assuming the process is legitimate, enters their PIN. Because a UPI PIN is exclusively used to deduct money, the funds are instantly drained from the student's bank account instead of credited.

\*\*Who is usually targeted:\*\* College students and young adults are frequent targets. They heavily rely on digital payments for daily expenses but may overlook critical security details when distracted or rushed.

\*\*What the consequences are:\*\* Consequences include severe, often unrecoverable financial loss—which can be devastating on a strict student budget—alongside a lasting breach of trust in digital platforms and significant emotional distress.
